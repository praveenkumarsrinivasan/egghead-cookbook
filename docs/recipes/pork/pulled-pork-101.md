# Pulled Pork 101

## Overview

An overnight dry-brined pork shoulder smoked double indirect and finished tender for easy pulling.

## Recipe Essentials

- Recipe type: Smoked pork shoulder
- Cuisine: American BBQ
- Prep time: 20 minutes + overnight dry brine
- Cook time: low and slow (varies)
- Total time: 1-2 days
- Serves: 8-12
- EGG setup: double indirect
- EGG target temperature: low and slow

## Ingredients

Pork:

- 1 pork shoulder

Dry brine:

- 2 Tbsp kosher salt
- 2 tsp white sugar
- 3/4 tsp paprika
- 1/4 tsp ground turmeric
- 1/4 tsp onion powder
- 1/4 tsp garlic powder
- 1/4 tsp cornstarch

Carolina sauce:

- 1/4 cup apple cider vinegar
- 1/4 cup ketchup
- 2 Tbsp brown sugar
- 1 Tbsp Worcestershire sauce
- 1 tsp salt
- 1 tsp black pepper
- 1/2 tsp garlic powder
- 1/2 tsp onion powder
- 1/2 tsp paprika
- 1/4 tsp cayenne pepper

Optional: Lawry's alternative

- 2 Tbsp kosher salt
- 2 tsp white sugar
- 3/4 tsp paprika
- 1/4 tsp ground turmeric
- 1/4 tsp onion powder
- 1/4 tsp garlic powder
- 1/4 tsp cornstarch

## Method

1. Dry brine the pork shoulder overnight.
2. Set up the EGG for a clean double-indirect fire and smoke low and slow.
3. When the bark is set, use a foil boat to help push through the stall.
4. Rest well (FTC method works) and pull.
5. Toss with Carolina sauce to taste.

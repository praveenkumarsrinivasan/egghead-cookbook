# Steak 101

## Overview

A reverse-sear steak method with overnight dry brine, a quick sear sauce, and optional Alabama white sauce.

## Recipe Essentials

- Recipe type: Reverse-sear steak
- Cuisine: American BBQ
- Prep time: 15 minutes + overnight dry brine
- Cook time: 30-60 minutes (varies by thickness)
- Total time: 1-2 days
- Serves: depends on steak size
- EGG setup: indirect, then direct for sear
- EGG target temperature: low then high heat for sear

## Ingredients

Steak:

- 1 ribeye or preferred steak, 1.5 inches thick
- Hot sauce for binder under dry rub

Dry brine:

- 2 Tbsp kosher salt
- 2 tsp white sugar
- 3/4 tsp paprika
- 1/4 tsp ground turmeric
- 1/4 tsp onion powder
- 1/4 tsp garlic powder
- 1/4 tsp cornstarch

Sear sauce:

- 2 Tbsp butter, melted
- 1 Tbsp fresh minced garlic
- 1/2 tsp black pepper
- 1/2 tsp kosher salt
- 1/2 tsp onion powder
- 1 tsp Worcestershire sauce
- 1 tsp soy sauce

Alabama white sauce (optional):

- 1 cup mayonnaise
- 1/4 cup apple cider vinegar
- 2 Tbsp Dijon mustard
- 1 Tbsp prepared horseradish
- 1 tsp kosher salt
- 1 tsp black pepper
- 1 tsp cayenne pepper

## Method

1. Dry brine steak overnight.
2. Set up the EGG for reverse sear and cook indirectly until just shy of target doneness.
3. Mix sear sauce and Alabama white sauce.
4. Sear over direct high heat, brushing with sear sauce.
5. Rest briefly and serve with Alabama white sauce.

## Notes

- Steps are summarized from the Steak 101 page.
- Adjust final temps to your preferred doneness.

# Brisket 101

## Overview

A repeatable brisket approach built around overnight dry brine, tallow injection, and a steady double-indirect smoke.

## Recipe Essentials

- Recipe type: BBQ brisket
- Cuisine: Texas-style BBQ
- Prep time: 30 minutes + overnight dry brine
- Cook time: low and slow (varies by size)
- Total time: 1-2 days
- Serves: depends on brisket size
- EGG setup: double indirect
- EGG target temperature: low and slow

## Ingredients

Brisket:

- 1 beef brisket

Dry brine:

- 1 Tbsp granulated garlic
- 2 Tbsp + 2 tsp kosher salt
- 4 tsp Lawry's (or copycat, see below)
- 4 Tbsp ground black pepper

Injection:

- 2 cups water
- Beef fat trimmings from brisket

Optional: Lawry's alternative

- 2 Tbsp kosher salt
- 2 tsp white sugar
- 3/4 tsp paprika
- 1/4 tsp ground turmeric
- 1/4 tsp onion powder
- 1/4 tsp garlic powder
- 1/4 tsp cornstarch

## Method

1. Trim brisket and reserve fat for tallow.
2. Apply the dry brine and rest overnight.
3. Render fat with water for a light tallow injection; inject the flat.
4. Set up the EGG for a clean, double-indirect fire and stabilize low and slow.
5. Smoke, adjusting airflow based on probe data and bark development.
6. Use a foil boat once the bark is set to push through the stall.
7. Rest thoroughly before slicing.

## Notes

- Method steps are condensed from the Brisket 101 page and its cooking plan.
- Adjust temps and timing to your brisket size and cooker behavior.
